Trading Strategies

Original strategies using proprietary indicators coupled with Tradingview and python frameworks

Fees included

Strategies include fees to provide realistic results. Without fees a strategy can have unrealistic results.

No repainting

Repainting happens when a strategy uses future data (values from closed candles) from higher timeframe on lower timeframe.

Live Tested

Strategies are tested on live account to check that the backtests are reliable. Direct exchange API connection are used.

No Hidden Drawdown

backtests avoid issues of hidden drowdawn that occur when backtests take into account the drowdawn only on closed bars.

No over-optimisation

The strategies avoid using different strategy settings for tested assets to protect from the issue of over-optimisation

Long term backtests

Backtests are performed on several years of data to test the strategies on bull and bear market.

Volatility Grid Strategy

VGS combines grid trading with a focus on volatility. It’s tailored for the cryptocurrency market, where prices move quickly and often. This strategy is about making those moves work for you, using tools that simplify the process.

Relative Volatility Strategy

The Relative Volatility Strategy (RVS) utilizes the differences in volatility between indicators or assets to make informed predictions about future price movements of an asset. It aims to find reversal entries.
